Music for Instagram

On further reflection of the ideas in ‘Digital Tarkovsky’, I have been thinking about how to creatively apply/address ideas around time and internet platforms.

Instagram is regularly discussed as the Social Networking platform that is the most detrimental to our mental health (check here, here, and here, and here.)

I want to experiment creating a music that is more suited to the platforms that competing for our attention, perhaps as a relief to the type of content that contributes to the negative aspects of the platform.

For this to work I would need to analyse the mechanics of the platform, and adapt the music I make to suit the medium and the ways in which people engage with content on there.

This type of music might need to be shorter in duration, to accommodate the increasingly shorter attention spans of users when on their smartphones.  

I’ll be producing my own Instagram works under the project name ‘flow’, a theme that is prevalent in ‘Digital Tarkovsky’. “We experience time as something that flows.”

By embracing loop-based works they edge into the world of the non-linear, they could either last forever, or less than one full cycle; the duration of the piece is decided by the person listening as they scroll through their Instagram feed.

These works will have their own ‘flow’ that exist alongside our own.
